# crtfyStudent Demo Scripts By Package

## Package 1: Operations

Demo goal: show an admissions team moving from queue visibility to student-specific action.

Flow:
1. Open Today's Work and explain prioritized buckets.
2. Search for a student.
3. Open Student 360.
4. Review identity, owner, source, stage, readiness, checklist, and transcript summary.
5. Open checklist and mark one item complete or needs review.
6. Show the Student 360 timeline entry created by the action.
7. Open document queue and exception summary.
8. Show admin role and checklist template configuration.
9. End on reporting overview.

Proof points:
- One record replaces separate CRM context switching.
- Work is prioritized by blocker and readiness.
- Staff writes are auditable.

## Package 2: Decision Intelligence

Demo goal: show how document and academic evidence become a controlled decision packet.

Flow:
1. Open Student 360 for a transfer student.
2. Show transcript evidence and transfer summary.
3. Open trust detail and explain block or clear controls.
4. Open decision studio.
5. Review packet evidence, credit estimate, recommendation, and timeline.
6. Run or review recommendation.
7. Hold, reopen, or release decision depending on gate status.
8. Show audit trail.

Proof points:
- Release is gated.
- Evidence is traceable.
- Sensitive academic and trust detail is permission-aware.

## Package 3: Yield And Handoff

Demo goal: show admitted-student risk, milestone blockers, and handoff readiness.

Flow:
1. Open Yield queue.
2. Filter by risk, owner, program, or deposit status.
3. Open an admitted student.
4. Log a yield intervention or follow-up.
5. Open Deposit/Melt queue.
6. Update a deposit or milestone status.
7. Open handoff status and package.
8. Show sync errors with retry and acknowledge.

Proof points:
- Teams can see why admitted students need attention.
- Cross-office blockers are explicit.
- Handoff risk is retryable and auditable.

## Package 4: Platform

Demo goal: show administrative control, integration readiness, and implementation tracking.

Flow:
1. Open connectors.
2. Show connector status and detail.
3. Connect or test a connector.
4. Review field mappings.
5. Open sync runs or sync errors.
6. Open implementation readiness.
7. Update checklist item status.
8. Open operational, outcomes, benchmarks, and drilldown reporting.

Proof points:
- Admins can see connector health.
- Mappings are configurable.
- Implementation has an explicit go-live path.
